Historical Sites

Batu Bersurat Memorial

The Batu Bersurat Terengganu, discovered in 1902 in Kuala Berang, Terengganu, is Malaysia's oldest Islamic artifact. Dating back to 1303 CE (702 Hijrah), it features Jawi inscriptions outlining the introduction of Islamic law in the Malay Peninsula. This historic stone symbolizes the early spread of Islam in the region and is now displayed at the Terengganu State Museum.

Haji Su Heritage House

Haji Su Heritage House is a traditional Malay wooden house located in Kampung Losong, Kuala Terengganu, Malaysia. This beautifully preserved house showcases authentic Terengganu architecture and serves as a cultural landmark reflecting the rich heritage of the region. Visitors can explore the intricate craftsmanship, traditional furnishings, and artifacts that tell the story of Terengganu’s cultural history. It is a perfect destination for those interested in experiencing the charm of Malaysia’s traditional lifestyle.

Tengku Anjang

The Tengku Anjang House is a historic traditional wooden house located in Terengganu, Malaysia. Known for its unique Malay architectural design, it features intricately carved panels and traditional craftsmanship that reflect the rich cultural heritage of Terengganu. The house stands as a testament to the region’s historical significance and serves as an important cultural landmark for visitors seeking to explore the beauty of Terengganu’s traditional lifestyle and architecture.

Bukit Puteri

Bukit Puteri is a historical hill located near the Terengganu River in Kuala Terengganu, Malaysia. Standing 200 meters above sea level, it was once a strategic fort during the 19th century, serving as a defense point and royal retreat. Today, Bukit Puteri is a popular tourist destination, featuring historical relics such as a cannon, a flagpole, a bell, and a lighthouse. Visitors can enjoy panoramic views of Kuala Terengganu while exploring its rich history and cultural significance.

Bewah Cave Archaeological Site

The Bewah Cave Archaeological Site, located within Tasik Kenyir in Terengganu, Malaysia, is a significant prehistoric site. This limestone cave is renowned for its archaeological discoveries, including ancient artifacts and human skeletal remains estimated to be over 16,000 years old. The cave features stunning stalactites and stalagmites, making it a fascinating destination for history enthusiasts and nature lovers. Visitors can explore its rich history and natural beauty, offering a glimpse into Malaysia’s prehistoric past.

Kota Lama Duyong

Kota Lama Duyong is a historic fortified house located on Pulau Duyong, Kuala Terengganu, Malaysia. Built in the 19th century, this unique structure combines traditional Malay and colonial architectural styles, featuring intricate wood carvings and sturdy brickwork. Once a center for Islamic learning and trade, it is now a cultural landmark that showcases Terengganu’s rich heritage. Visitors can explore its historical significance, traditional craftsmanship, and serene surroundings.

Bidong Island

Bidong Island, located off the coast of Terengganu, Malaysia, is a small, picturesque island with a deep historical significance. Once used as a refugee camp for Vietnamese boat people from 1978 to 1991, it now stands as a memorial site. Visitors can explore remnants of the refugee settlement, including temples, monuments, and artifacts, while also enjoying the island's natural beauty with crystal-clear waters, coral reefs, and white sandy beaches. Bidong Island offers a unique blend of history and tranquility for those seeking a meaningful and scenic getaway
